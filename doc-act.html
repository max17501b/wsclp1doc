WSC-LP1 User Manual

Operation

Activities

The Activities Page shows the status of your enabled watering zones and the activity currently queued to run. You can turn zones on and off. You can pause, resume and purge the work queue. And you can view the Activity Log.

Zone Status

The zone status display lists all your enabled zones and their current status. The icon signifies the zone is off. The icon signifies the zone is on.

Turning zone on & off

You can manually turn zones on and off clicking or tapping the zone status icon. Turning off an active zone removes its entry from the work queue. The next job in line becomes active.

Turning on an inactive zone puts a new job entry at the front of the work queue. The job runs for 60 second. Any current activity is postponed. Normal operation resumes when the newly inserted job finishes.

Work Queue

The Work Queue is a list of watering activity ready to go. Each entry in the queue comprises a zone and a duration. When an entry reaches the top of the queue, that zone is turned on and left on for the specified duration.

Entries get into the work queue in several ways:

  • When the start time of a scheduled program is reached the program is divided into entries and inserted in the queue.
  • If you manually start a program it is divided into entries and inserted in the queue.
  • If you manually start or test a zone a single 60-second entry is inserted at the front of the queue.

Managing the Work Queue

The work queue display includes one or more buttons on the right margin which you can use to manage the queue.

Click this button to pause the work queue. All activity stops and remains in queue.
Click this button to resume the work queue. Activity resumes with the top-most entry.
Click this button to clear the work queue. All pending entries are removed. There is no "undo" for this action.

You use the pause/resume sequence to temporarily stop watering. Maybe you want to fix a sprinkler head or someone is working in the area and doesn't need watering.

Use the clear button the immediately stop and remove all queued work. You might use this if a program got started by accident, or the forecast changes and you don't want to finish an already-scheduled program.

The Activity Log

Click the Activity Log button to view the latest watering activity. The date, time, zone and duration of each zone operation is listed, in reverse-time order. The report includes 75 of the most-recent watering events. Depending on your watering schedule this may span several weeks.

If you find a zone has been isolated or automatically disabled, look in this report and you should see one or more records listing the zone as "DNF". This means the zone was started but did not finish, indicative of an over-current shutdown of the controller.
