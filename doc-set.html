WSC-LP1 User Manual

Operation

Settings

The settings page configures the name, timezone, zone details and other miscellaneous features of the WSC-LP1 controller. You should review these settings once when the controller is first set up, and you'll rarely have to visit this page again.

Controller Name

Type a name for your controller. This name will identify the controller on your WIFI network and in your browser.

If you have multiple WSC-LP1 controllers make sure each has a unique name. This makes it easier to distinguish them on your WIFI network, and in your browser.

Your WIFI router may restrict names to alpha-numeric characters, and the dash and underscore characters. So don't go overboard with exotic names unless you know your network accepts them.

Time Zone

Select the time zone for your controller.

In more detail, make sure the controller time zone is the same as your browser time zone. As far as I can tell, modern browsers get their time zone from their host operating system, and you cannot do much to change it. So it's probably safe to say the WSC-LP1 time zone should be the same as the PC/phone/whatever which hosts your browser.

If the WSC-LP1 time zone is out of sync with your browser time zone things can get confusing. So try to keep them the same.

Zones

The WSC-LP1 supports up to six sprinkler solenoids, called "zones". The settings page lists each zone along with its status, description and a setup button.

The zone status icon

Zone status is indicated by these icons:

Zone is ready, good to go.
Zone is isolated. A zone is isolated if it is suspected of drawing excessive current. Isolated zones are taken out of the normal program order and run after all other zones have finished.
Zone is disabled. Disabled zones are removed from normal operations. They can only be run via the setup test procedure.

Changing zone status

To change a zones status click or tap on the zone icon. Clicking a ready zone disables it. Clicking an isolated zone disables it. Clicking a disabled zone readies it.

Using the Setup Button

Click or tap the Setup button to configure the description, order and wire color for a zone. Setup also allows testing disabled zones. For more details see Zone Setup Procedures.

Dead Time

When the controller switches from one sprinkler zone to another, there are good reasons to pause for a few seconds. Many of your consumer-grade sprinkler valves rely on water pressure to fully seat the valve in a closed position. A short pause before starting the new zone allows full pressure to develop and helps ensure valves seat properly.

Another benefit involves the triac devices which switch A/C power to the valve solenoids. Traics do not switch off instantly. A short pause before starting a new zone ensures all triacs are fully off before the new zone switches on.

Select a dead time between 1 and 10 seconds. On my own system I use 8 seconds. It takes about 5-6 seconds for my valves to shut off.

Common Wire Color

Record the color of your COM wire here. If you ever have to move or re-wire your controller you will appreciate knowing which is the COM wire.
