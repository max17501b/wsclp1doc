WSC-LP1 User Manual

Troubleshooting

WIFI

Weak Signals

The WSC-LP1 radio operates very well with signal levels -89dBm and higher. From -90dBm to -93dBm operation degrades, but the controller is usually functional. Signals weaker than -93dBm will cause disconnects and interruption of the web server interface.

If you're having trouble with signal strength, here are some things to try.

Location location location

Small changes in the location of the WSC-LP1 can affect signal strength, especially at extended ranges. Unmount the unit, power it with a battery, and monitor the WIFI signal strength as you move it around. Mount it where you have the best reception.

Similarly, if you have the option of moving your WIFI router, monitor the WIFI signal strength while you move the router. Place the router where you get the best performance.

Try different channels

The WIFI b/g/n spectrum is divided into 11 channels. Most routers automatically select a channel, and it's usually channel 5. If you have neighbors, and their routers are also in auto mode (highly likely) then channel 5 will be crowded.

If your router provides the option, try channels 11 or 10. They work best in my crowded WIFI space.

In theory the lowest channels have the best chance of penetrating walls. Didn't work for me, but give it a try.

Use a narrow bandwidth

If your router has the option, use the narrowest bandwidth possible. This is a tradeoff between range and speed: a wide bandwidth has less range but higher speed; a narrow bandwidth has better range but less speed. If your WIFI network is streaming high volume data like movies, you may want to stick with wide bandwidth.

No substitute for horsepower

Last resort, consider boosting your signal. There are numerous options, too many to recommend here.
