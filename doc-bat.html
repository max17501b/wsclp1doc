WSC-LP1 User Manual

Installation

Temporary Power

The WSC-LP1 can be temporarily powered with a 9-volt battery in order to configure the WIFI radio.

What you need

  • A 9-volt battery with a two-wire "snap connector"
  • Your trusty WSC-LP1 controller

Powering the WSC-LP1

The WSC-LP1 cannot operate valve solenoids using battery power. Do not connect valve wiring, or attempt to activate zones while under battery power.

The battery is connected to the 3-terminal A/C power connector on the lower left side of the controller unit, as shown above. Use a common 9V "snap connector". Secure the red lead to the left screw terminal, and the black lead to right terminal. Leave the middle terminal unconnected.

As soon as power is applied the 24V power indicator LED should light, and the status LED should flash.

If the LEDs fail to light as described disconnect the battery and check that the wiring polarity is correct (red to (+) black to (-)) and check that the battery is fresh.

The controller draws about 25mA. A fresh alkaline battery should provide better than 12 hours operation.
